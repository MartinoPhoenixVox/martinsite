## A. PERSONALITY

### 11

The principal goal of personality formation is human maturity. Although the candidacy program is only a beginning, it is necessary and beneficial insofar as it helps candidates become aware of, and actively cultivate, fundamental human values.

Candidates strive to develop the following:

#### 11a

Sound intelligence: They learn to analyze, synthesize, and integrate information in order to form sound judgment. The more candidates develop a well-formed conscience and right judgment, the more effectively they are able to serve.

#### 11b

A stable will: They seek truth and goodness with perseverance and determination, maintaining clarity of purpose in striving toward their goals.

#### 11c

Emotional balance: In the complex realm of passions and emotions present in every person, candidates are helped to become aware of their inner movements and to learn how to integrate and channel them toward growth. Above all, candidates of the Society of Jesus are called to be joyful and happy persons, whose faith and inner joy radiate naturally and attractively to others.

### 12

Good health is essential for study and for the service of God. Candidates are therefore expected to be attentive to the care and development of their physical health. One important means of maintaining and improving health is regular physical exercise and participation in sports during designated free time in the candidacy schedule.

### 13

If a candidate is unable to participate in common recreation or physical activities due to other responsibilities, he must inform the Father Director accordingly.

### 14

Should a candidate become aware of unhealthy habits or conditions related to study, diet, sleep, or other aspects of daily life, he is to inform the Father Director promptly so that timely adjustments can be made.

### 15

Whenever a candidate experiences unusual health conditions or becomes ill, he must inform the Father Director as soon as possible.

## B. SPIRITUALITY

### 16

Spiritual formation is the highest priority of the candidate's way of life. Through the grace of the Holy Spirit, candidates hear the call of Jesus: "Follow me." They respond generously and are ready to renounce all in order to follow the Lord. This call and respond must be rooted in a deep and personal love.

### 17

Candidates therefore learn to "know" Christ not only intellectually, but also through a close and loving relationship. They renew this love through faithful prayer, discernment, daily participation in the Eucharist, and regular reading of Sacred Scripture.

### 18

Each candidate strives to fulfill the prescribed spiritual practices faithfully, punctually, and at an appropriate level, according to his conscience before God.

### 19

If, for any reason, a candidate is unable to complete a spiritual exercise at the scheduled time, he should make every effort to complete it at another suitable time. Should a change in the schedule be necessary, he is to inform the Father Director.

### 20

Recognizing the spiritual benefits of the Sacrament of Reconciliation, each candidate goes to confession at least once a month.

### 21

In order to discern God's will regarding the consecration of his life and the pursuit of his vocation, each candidate meets with his spiritual director for spiritual conversation at least once a month. In addition, there is a monthly recollection, and each year a period of Spiritual Exercises is provided, so that candidates may grow in self-knowledge, deepen their interior life, and live in closer intimacy with God.

## C. COMMUNITY

### 22

Community life is essential for candidates, since they are being prepared to enter the Society of Jesus, an apostolic body. Therefore, candidates are expected to develop the capacity to live in community.

### 23

A candidate who possesses the ability to live in community understands that he is called to live for others. "Others" meads everyone: those who are different from him, who have weaknesses, or even those who may oppose him. Candidates learn to cultivate concrete, generous, open, and spiritually grounded friendships.

### 24

The fundamental elements of community life are trustworthy fidelity and humble service.

### 25

Candidates are to take an active role in building a community rooted in faith. At the same time, they should avoid attitudes and behaviors that harm community life, such as superficiality, lack of discipline, self-satisfaction, and isolation.

### 26

When there is no Father Director or assistant residing in the community, candidates should view this situation as an opportunity to grow in maturity according to the priciple of "self-formation." Nevertheless, they are to meet regularly with the Father Director and reflect honestly on community life, so that he may provide appropriate guidance.

### 27

Each house of candidacy must maintain an appropriate atmosphere of silence, ensuring favorable conditions for prayer, study, and rest, both for individuals and for the community as a whole.

### 28

Each candidate is expected to exercise a high degree of personal responsibility in observing the common schedule and his individual timetable as approved by the Father Director. Should a candidate need to modify the schedule, he must present the request to the Father Director for approval.

### Functions

#### 29

In order to foster community life and to strengthen the spirit of service and responsibility, candidates undertake various duties and daily activities in the House of Candidacy, such as those related to liturgy, education, sports, hygiene, and other common services.

#### 30

In accordance with the principle of "individual responsibility" and "collective collaboration," the Directorate appoints Candidate Number One and Candidate Number Two for the House of Candidacy as a whole, as well as for each community in particular. Other functions are proposed by the candidates and confirmed by the Directorate.

### 31

The common functions of the House of Candidacy are held for a period of one year and are handed over from the outgoing candidate to the incoming one. Community-level functions are held for a period of six months and are likewise handed over.

#### 31a

Candidate Number One: Has overall responsibility for community life; maintains contact with the Directorate to coordinate activities; guides the program and lifestyle of the candidacy community; handles external correspondence; and keeps the community diary.

#### 31b

Candidate Number Two: Replaces Candidate Number One when necessary; oversees tools, food and drink, maintenance, and the distribution of daily assignments; and keeps records of community income and expenses.
