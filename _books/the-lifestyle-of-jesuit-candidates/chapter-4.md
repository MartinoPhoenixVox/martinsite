## A. COMMUNICATIONS

### 64

The candidate should learn how to live within and to empathize with the world around him in order to offer everything to God. At the same time, he must ensure that nothing compromises his vocation or his duties.

### 65

In his communication with others, the candidate should be friendly, open, and prudent. He should be conscious of belonging to God, wise in speech and conduct, and careful in judgment and discernment before accepting anything. Proper attitude is also expressed through neat and appropriate dress. At the same time, he should be warm, courteous, polite, natural, and cheerful, giving witness to a balanced and joyful life in God.

### 66

Humility, affability, sympathy, attentiveness, and a readiness to serve others in the spirit of Christian charity are key virtues that draw human hearts to God.

### 67

Those responsible for guiding the candidate can help him develop sound judgment and prudent attitudes if he learns to open his heart and mind sincerely about what he has seen and heard-experiences that may raise questions, confusion, admiration, or affection within him. Such sincere openness is essential at every stage of formation.

### 68

The candidate should write regularly to his parents, relatives, and benefactors, inquiring about their well-being and sharing appropriate news about himself. He should do so only during his free time and avoid unnecessary details that could cause difficulties for his vocation.

## B. HOLIDAYS

### 69

The candidate is ordinarily permitted to return home on special occasions, such as the Vietnamese Lunar New Year and summer vacation. He may also visit his family at other times when this is appropriate and necessary. On such occasions, the candidate should give particular attention to nurturing his spiritual life and the practice of virtue.

### 70

Rest does not mean inactivity, but rather a change of daily activities for greater effectiveness in the future. Holidays should be regarded as special times for strengthening self-awareness and personal responsibility, and thus for growth in emotional and human maturity. Moreover, holidays offer good opportunities for the candidate to renew and exercise a missionary spirit through prayer, good example, and service to others.
