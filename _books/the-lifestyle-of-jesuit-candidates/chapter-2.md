## 8

The formation of candidates cannot be the responsibility of one person alone, but of many. Three principal agents may be identified.

First, the Holy Spirit, who takes the initiative in enlightening, moving, and strengthening the Jesuit vocation within the candidate. The candidate himself, therefore, should always be grateful for this grace and respond with a humble, honest, wholesome life, ready to cooperate with the Holy Spirit in his own formation.

Second, the candidate himself. He must be conscious that he is a key agent in his formation and bears primary responsibility for it. He is not to live passively, but to cooperate actively with the formator at all times. He freely accepts the specific lifestyle of a Jesuit candidate, guided by the principle of "self-formation."

Third, the formator. He should always be aware that he is a co-worker with the Holy Spirit and a companion to the candidate. Accordingly, he carries out his duty with a spiritual, loving, and discerning attitude, helping the candidate and accompanying him in the search for God's will.

## 9

The principle "self-formation"
First of all, the candidate should bear in mind that "self-formation" is entirely different from, and even contrary to, solitary formation that minimizes the need for guidance from a formator.

The principle of "self-formation" necessarily presupposes a fundamental conviction at a certain moment, the candidate clearly recognizes God's loving call addressed to him. He freely responds to this call and desires to live all that the vocation requires. He cherishes the vocation as a most precious gift that God has reserved for him personally. Consequently, he makes every effort to preserve and manifest this vocation with enthusiasm and initiative, sustained by an inner attitude of peaceful joy.

Embracing "self-formation" also entails a voluntary commitment to live according to the specific regulations of the candidate's way of life. The candidate strives to correct habitual faults and fulfills his duties not out of external pressure or for the sake of others, but out of love for God, which urges him forward and brings him joy as he grows in his vocation.

Any candidate who truly practices "self-formation" must live consistently and honestly, with an undivided heart-whether alone, in community with his brothers, in society, or in the presence of the formator. He must always remain attentive and receptive to the inspirations of the Holy Spirit acting in a sincere conscience, a well as to the guidance offered by the formator.

## 10

The candidate maintains regular contact and exchanges with the Father Director regarding matters related to health, diet, studies, personal and community schedules, finances, apostolic activities, correspondence, and communication. Concerning matters of interior spiritual life, the candidate consults the Spiritual Director.
