## 1

A Jesuit candidate is a young man who has been approved by the Society of Jesus to discern and respond to the loving call of our Lord Jesus Christ, inviting him to offer his entire life in the service of the Kingdom, in accordance with Jesuit spirituality. Through God's grace, he may be admitted into the Society and proceed on the path toward becoming a Jesuit.

## 2

A Jesuit is a man who is deeply aware that he is a sinner yet forgiven and called by God to be a companion of Jesus.

## 3

The purpose of the Society of Jesus is to follow our Lord Jesus Christ, carrying the Cross, and to be willingly sent by His Vicar on earth wherever He may chooose, and to do whatever may be required, for the greater glory of God and the more effective service of men and women today, in conformity with the spirit of contemplation in action.

## 4

The goal of the House of Candidacy is to provide appropriate opportunities and facilities for young men who desire to pursue a Jesuit vocation, in preparation for their more generous service to God and the Church.

## 5

The House of Candidacy bears the responsibility of providing its candidates with a comprehensive formation program; that is, to foster the development of the candidate's capacities in the human, spiritual, communal, intellectual, and apostolic dimensions.

## 6

The Holy Family of Jesus is the most vivid model for the Jesuit House of Candidacy. Before His public ministry, Jesus lived for many years under the roof of Nazareth, and this period bore rich fruit: He grew and became strong, filled with wisdom, and the favor of God and of His neighbors was upon Him.

## 7

Today, candidates seek the mystery of the hidden life of Jesus within the context of the House of Candidacy; there they learn to live under the gaze of the loving Father. Taking up the example of Jesus the Nazarene, they strive to fulfill their daily duties in silence, love, and obedience. They desire that their will and heartsbe confromed to His, and all that they seek to do is for the service of the Father's Kingdom.
