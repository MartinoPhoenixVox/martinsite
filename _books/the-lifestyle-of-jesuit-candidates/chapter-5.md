## 71

The candidate's way of life is founded primarily on a sense of personal responsibility and self-awareness. Other elements, such as the directorate, the schedule, and the community atmosphere, provide secondary and external support.

## 72

The candidate should remain aware that without God's grace and assistance, all efforts are empty and ineffective, that God invites him to cooperate with Him through wisdom and upright conduct especially in his interactions with others; and that he will not always be able to see or clearly judge the fruits of his efforts, particularly in his spiritual and moral life. At times, he may think he has succeeded when he has not, and at other times the opposite may be true.

## 73

With this awareness, the candidate should persevere in practicing the virtues of faith, hope, and charity during this period of formation. He should faithfully engage in spiritual practices so that, even when faced with difficulties and trials, he may maintain a supernatural spirit of optimism and balance, overcome disappointment and frustration, and avoid complaints or negative reactions.

## 74

For the good and the fruitfulness of the Candidacy Program, the candidate should strive to acquire and live out the above-mentioned elements of the Lifestyle.

## 75

Accordingly, the candidate should read this booklet at each monthly recollection and, in the presence of God, examine his fidelity to his vocation discernment journey as expressed in this Lifestyle.

## 76
